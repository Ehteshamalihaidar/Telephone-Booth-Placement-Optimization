Telephone Booth Placement Optimization

Problem Statement

The objective is to place telephone booths across a city such that every house is within n meters of at least one booth. The goal is to determine the minimum number of booths needed and the optimal locations to ensure full coverage.

The challenge lies in satisfying the n-meter distance constraint for all houses while minimizing the number of booths. The city is represented as a grid or graph, with each node corresponding to a house, and the telephone booths are positioned strategically to cover the maximum number of houses with the least overlap.

Algorithm Identified (CSP/SEARCH)

This optimization problem requires balancing coverage and efficiency, ensuring that each house is within the defined proximity to a booth while using the fewest possible resources.

CSP Constraints:

  • Distance Constraint: Each house must be within n meters of at least one telephone booth.
  • Minimization Objective: Minimize the total number of booths while covering all houses.

Search Algorithm Options

  • Greedy or Heuristic Search Used to find a feasible solution by placing booths close to houses and minimizing the number of booths.
  • A* Algorithm: Explores different configurations of booth placements to minimize the total number while ensuring the coverage constraint is satisfied.
